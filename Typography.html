The Art of Typography in Web Design

Typography is more than just choosing a font — it’s about creating a visual rhythm and harmony that guides readers through content effortlessly. Good typography improves readability, evokes emotion, and enhances the overall experience of a website.

Web designers often combine multiple font families to establish hierarchy — for instance, using a bold sans-serif for headings and a lighter serif for body text. Proper use of font-weight and font-size can help differentiate important information from general content.

Adjusting line-height and letter-spacing makes long paragraphs more pleasant to read. Centered, left, or justified alignment can completely change the tone of an article. Designers also use subtle color contrasts to draw attention without overwhelming readers.

Finally, CSS pseudo-elements like ::first-letter and ::first-line allow for creative emphasis — similar to how printed books start paragraphs with decorative initials.
